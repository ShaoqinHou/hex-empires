## Identity

Navigable river — a wide water tile (new in Civ VII) that connects inland lakes to the coast. Traversable by naval units including Treasure Fleets. A major VII innovation: inland cities on navigable rivers have near-equal economic potential to coastal cities. Enemy naval units can raid these rivers, creating new threat vectors.

- Historical period: The Nile, Amazon, Yangtze, Mississippi — great navigable inland waterways
- Flavor: The inland naval highway; unique to Civ VII among strategy games.

## Stats / numeric attributes

- Base yield: +1 Food, +1 Gold
- Movement cost: Free for naval units; costs all remaining MP for land units (embark); bridges allow 1 MP crossing [INFERRED]
- Defense modifier: 0 (river tile itself; -5 CS to defender on minor river crossing by contrast)
- Combat rules: Naval units traverse freely; land units must embark (all MP); naval raids possible
- Can build: Both coastal AND land buildings for adjacent settled cities; port buildings [INFERRED]
